The country was in a superb state of defence. Every coast city had been well supplied with land fortifications; the army under the parental eye of the General <name>Staff</name>, organized according to the prussian system, had been increased to 300,000 men, with a territorial reserve of a million; and six magnificent squadrons of cruisers and battle-ships patrolled the six stations of the navigable seas, leaving a steam reserve amply fitted to control home waters.
